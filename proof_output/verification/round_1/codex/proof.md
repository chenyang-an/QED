# Proof

## Problem Statement
Compute what is 5th Fibonacci number.

## Proof
The Fibonacci sequence is generated by the rule that each term after the initial ones is the sum of the two preceding terms.

Under the standard convention
$$
F_1=1,\qquad F_2=1,\qquad F_n=F_{n-1}+F_{n-2}\quad (n\ge 3),
$$
we compute successively:
$$
F_3=F_2+F_1=1+1=2,
$$
$$
F_4=F_3+F_2=2+1=3,
$$
and
$$
F_5=F_4+F_3=3+2=5.
$$
Therefore, under this standard definition, the 5th Fibonacci number is $5$.

There is another common indexing convention,
$$
F_0=0,\qquad F_1=1,\qquad F_n=F_{n-1}+F_{n-2}\quad (n\ge 2).
$$
Using this convention,
$$
F_2=F_1+F_0=1+0=1,\qquad
F_3=F_2+F_1=1+1=2,\qquad
F_4=F_3+F_2=2+1=3,
$$
so
$$
F_5=F_4+F_3=3+2=5.
$$
Thus the requested value is $5$ in either standard convention.

## Key Ideas
Use the defining Fibonacci recurrence to compute the terms one by one until reaching the fifth term. The only possible ambiguity is indexing, and both standard conventions give the same value $5$.
