Consider the advection-diffusion equation on $\mathbb{T}^2 = [-\pi,\pi]^2$:
$$\partial_t \rho + U(t,y)\,\partial_x \rho = \Delta \rho,$$
where $U \in L^\infty_t L^2_y(\mathbb{T})$ is a shear flow and $\rho(0,\cdot) \in L^2(\mathbb{T}^2)$ is mean-zero and nonzero.

Define the mixing scale ratio
$$\Lambda(t) = \frac{\|\rho(t)\|_{\dot{H}^{-1}(\mathbb{T}^2)}}{\|\rho(t)\|_{L^2(\mathbb{T}^2)}}.$$

Prove that
$$\liminf_{t \to \infty} \Lambda(t) > 0,$$
i.e., the $\dot{H}^{-1}$ norm cannot decay faster than the $L^2$ norm — the Batchelor scale persists.
